About

Joining a community garden is a great way to eat healthier, reduce your environmental footprint, and learn about food production.

Community gardens also has benefits for children, youth, and society as a whole. Studies have unveiled that community gardens contribute to community building, crime reduction, health improvement, and mutual understanding.

How it Works

There are over 75 community gardens in Vancouver. These gardens are often located in city parks, school yards, and private property.

Get Involved

All community gardens are administered by local community groups. The City of Vancouver and City Staff do not oversee community gardens.

If you wish to start a plot in a garden near you, contact the garden administrators directly. Each individual contact information can be found online.

Start a Community Garden

The City and Park Board welcomes inividuals and community groups to initiate new urban agriculture projects in parks and on City-owned land.

If you have a site pre-selected that you think is ideal for a community garden or orchard, you are encouraged to contact the City and Park Board to begin! See below for application instructions.

Projects in City parks or City-owned land

You can fill out an expression of interest application if you wish to start your own project in a City park or on City-owned property.

Applications are accepted year-round and are reviewed each fall.


  1. Select and identify a site where you would like to start your community garden. To verify the ownership of the plot, call 3-1-1 to find out.
  2. Review the City of Vancouver's expectations for how projects should ideally be run and operated.
  3. Read over the Urban Agriculture Garden Guide to learn how you can start and design your project.
  4. Complete and submit an Expression of Interest Application.
  5. Email your Expression of Interest Application to foodpolicy@vancouver.ca.
  6. The City of Vancouver will review your application. Succesful applicants will be contacted to submit a full formal proposal.
  7. The City of Vancouver will assist you with the design and completion of your site preparations.
